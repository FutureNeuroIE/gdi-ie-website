What is the Genomic Data Infrastructure Ireland project?

Genomic Data Infrastructure (GDI) Ireland is the Irish node of the European GDI project which brings together experts across the European Union to form a secure network of human genetic data. The GDI project will realise the ambitions of the Beyond 1 Million Genomes (B1MG) project and the 1+Million Genomes (1+MG) initiative.

What data will be used?

Human genome data will be used in the GDI Ireland project. A genome is the entire set of DNA in one organism. Gathering all of this data in one place has the potential to better our understanding of disease, improve diagnoses and lead to new treatment options.

Who will access the data?

Our aim is to create safe and secure computer systems and protocols, so that Irish genetic data can be safely shared with approved clinicans, researchers and healthcare policy makers in Ireland and across Europe. Anyone with access to GDI Ireland data will require approval by a data access committee.

What are the benefits?

Benefits of genomic data sharing include improved and advanced research outputs, potential discovery of new treatment options, the development of new diagnostic tests and improved access to clinical trials. All of this together has the potential to greatly improve clinical care in Ireland in terms of patient outcomes and health economics.

Who is involved?

Researchers from four institutes in Ireland involved in the project at a national level. We are working with national agencies, research organisations, and technology providers nationally and across the European Union. The project is overseen by a diverse steering committee who will provide accountable and transparent governance.
